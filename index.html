Low-background steel

Since the start of the atomic bomb testing programmes the amount of carbon-14 in the atmosphere is a lot higher so radiocarbon dating1 will not work the same in the future.
There is also radioactive cobalt-60 particles in the atmosphere that contaminates new steel (since you use atmospheric air in the process of making steel) so for very sensitive instruments like geiger counters one has to use what’s called “low-background steel” that is made from old steel boats sunken before 1940.

  1. When nitrogen in the atmosphere is hit by cosmic rays it produces the radioactive element carbon-14. Some of that carbon-14 is combined with atmospheric oxygen producing carbon dioxide that is absorbed by plants through photosynthesis. Animals acquire the radioactive carbon when eating the plants.
    When a plant or animal dies it stops absorbing the radioactive carbon and the carbon-14 undergoes radioactive decay.
    Every 5730 years it looses half of its radioactivity and by measuring the amount of radioactivity of an old organic object it is possible to figure out when it died and stopped absorbing carbon-14 from the air.
