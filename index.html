Haber Bosch

The Haver Bosch process, invented by German chemists Fritz Haber and Carl Bosch, might be the most important invention of humanity. The process converts atmospheric nitrogen (N2) to ammonia (NH3) by a reaction with hydrogen (H2) using a metal catalyst under high temperatures and pressures.
The ammonia is then used to create synthetic fertilisers. About half the worlds population is currently fed as a result of synthetic nitrogen fertilisers.
