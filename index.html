British East India

British colony rule in India was not run by the British government but by a joint stock company called the East India Company1. The company rule began in 1757 and lasted until 1858 when the territory was ceded to the British government.

  1. At one point the company accounted for half the world trade, mostly in cotton, silk, indigo dye, salt, spices, saltpetre, tea, and importantly opium
