Joanne of Arc

Although very famous at the time she lived there is no known depiction or description of what Joanne of Arc1 looked like. At least not by anyone who actually saw her with their own eyes apart from the fact that she had short hair and wore men’s clothing.

The earliest known picture of her is from May 10, 1429 by Clément de Fauquembergue who, despite never meeting her, made a doodle of her from imagination in the margin of a parliamentary council register.

Joanne of Arc is also the only person that has been killed by the catholic church2 and later been canonizated by it3.

  1. French: Jeanne d’Arc, c. 1412 – 30 May 1431

  2. Burnt on the stake for heresy in 1431

  3. 16 May 1920 by Pope Benedict XV
