Joanne of Arc

There is no known depiction or description of what Joanne of Arc1 looked like by anyone who saw her with their own eyes from when she was alive.

The earliest known picture of her is from May 10, 1429 by Clément de Fauquembergue who, dispite never meeting her, made a doodle of her from imagination in the margin of the parliamentary council register.

Joanne of Arc is also the only one that has been killed by the catholic church and later been canonizated by it2.

  1. French: Jeanne d’Arc, c. 1412 – 30 May 1431

  2. 16 May 1920 by Pope Benedict XV
